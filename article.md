### Chasing Giants at the Rush Springs Watermelon Festival
If there's one thing the residents of Rush Springs, Oklahoma take
seriously, it's their watermelons. For over 70 years, this small town
has played host to the annual Watermelon Festival, a celebration of the
community's agricultural heritage and a showcase for some of the
largest, juiciest melons you'll find anywhere in the world.

In 1965, Rush Springs added a competition for the largest watermelon.
The first winner: 60-pounder. Think about that. If you go to Walmart and
buy a large watermelon, it is probably 20--25 lbs. 60 lbs is the size of
an average first grader.

And that was just the beginning. Over the years, the winning watermelons
have grown. And grown.


<figcaption>Data on the winning watermelons since 1965.</figcaption>


In 1983, the team of Mark Williams and Allen Heilman set a new festival
record with a 142-pound melon. A high water(melon) mark that would last
until 2004 when Taylor Tumblson raised the bar with a 146-pound melon.
Tumblson has taken the blue ribbon a record 10 times.


<figcaption>Weight of the winning watermelon over time in pounds:
1965–2024</figcaption>


But the story doesn't end there. In 2023, a new champion
emerged --- Garrin Bratcher, whose 225-pound watermelon set a new
all-time festival record. And this year, 2024, the Bratcher Farms team
claimed the top spot with a 268-pound melon. If this melon were a boxer,
it would be in the super heavyweight class, the same class as Mike
Tyson. Tyson only weighs 262 lbs.


<figcaption>Photo by author.</figcaption>


What is it about Rush Springs that has allowed it to cultivate such
remarkable watermelons over the years? The answer lies in the town's
geography and climate, which are ideal for growing this beloved fruit.
Located in southwestern Oklahoma, Rush Springs enjoys a long growing
season, ample sunshine, nutrient-rich soil, and abundant watermelon
love --- all the ingredients necessary for producing giant watermelons.

The town's residents have embraced the watermelon as a source of
community pride, rallying around the annual festival and the growers who
push the boundaries of what's possible. From the watermelon-eating
contests to the ever-growing display of massive melons, the festival has
become a cherished tradition that brings the town together in
celebration of its agricultural heritage. They even crown an annual
watermelon queen.

In Oklahoma, the Rush Springs Watermelon Festival is a summertime
ritual, a chance to indulge in the sweet, juicy fruit and soak up the
vibrant, family-friendly atmosphere --- plus play some carnival games.

The festival shows no signs of slowing down as it enters its eighth
decade. Growers continue to push the limits of what's possible and the
community remains as committed to preserving this beloved tradition.

Just imagine the sheer thrill of standing before a Mike Tyson-sized
268-pound watermelon, the product of months of painstaking care and
cultivation.

Whether you're a seasoned festival-goer or a first-time visitor, you're
sure to be amazed by the sheer size of the watermelons and the
creativity of the watermelon themed attractions and outfits.

Come on down, sink your teeth into a juicy slice, and join in the
celebration of this Oklahoma treasure.
